# Go-To-Market

The exact target user is a finance/ops lead or engineering manager at a 10–80 person startup that adopted AI tools quickly and now has messy recurring spend across Cursor, ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, and API accounts. This person is not looking for a full procurement suite. They are usually preparing for a renewal, a budget review, or a founder asking why the software bill keeps growing.

Right before they want ModelMeter, they are probably searching or scrolling things like: "Cursor team pricing worth it," "ChatGPT Business vs Plus," "how much should startups spend on AI tools," "Claude Team pricing," "OpenAI API cost control," or reading posts about AI tool sprawl. The emotional trigger is not curiosity about AI; it is irritation with recurring SaaS spend and uncertainty about whether the team is overpaying.

Specific channels for the first 100 users:

- r/startups and r/SaaS posts framed around "how much are you spending on AI tools per employee?"
- Indie Hackers and FounderPal-style communities with a free audit angle
- X replies to founders posting about SaaS bills, AI agents, or Cursor/Claude/ChatGPT costs
- engineering manager communities such as Rands Leadership Slack or LeadDev-adjacent groups, where the angle is "are we double-paying for coding assistants?"
- small finance/operator communities where the PDF export matters more than the novelty of AI

With $0 paid budget, I would spend 30 days doing founder-led distribution:

1. Publish a short launch post with three example audits: solo developer, 12-person startup, API-heavy team.
2. DM 50 founders/operators with a specific question: "Do you know your AI spend per employee?"
3. Post a simple benchmark thread: "AI tools per developer: what $20, $60, and $100/month actually buys."
4. Offer to manually review the first 25 serious reports and turn the best anonymized patterns into a public teardown.
5. Ask every user who downloads a PDF to share the report internally and reply with one missing tool/provider.

The unfair Credex channel is inventory knowledge. Credex knows where discounted AI credits exist and which tools have real savings supply. ModelMeter can surface demand: teams with high modeled savings, specific vendor overlap, and renewal pain. That is more targeted than generic ads.

Week-1 traction if this works: 150 audit starts, 80 completed audits, 20 lead captures, 8 PDF downloads, 5 high-savings reports, and 2 Credex consultation requests. If completion is high but lead capture is low, the report is useful but the capture offer is weak. If audit starts are low, distribution/copy is the issue.

# Economics

This is a rough unit economics model for Credex using ModelMeter as a lead-generation asset.

Assumption: Credex sells discounted AI infrastructure credits or access routes that create meaningful savings for teams already spending heavily on AI tools. A converted buyer might purchase or broker `$10k-$50k` in annual credits depending on team size and usage. If Credex earns an effective gross margin or fee of 10–20%, a converted customer could be worth roughly `$1k-$10k` gross profit in year one. For a conservative model, I would use `$3,000` gross profit per converted customer.

Funnel assumptions:

- 1,000 visitors
- 450 audit starts
- 300 completed audits
- 75 lead captures
- 15 high-savings leads (`>$500/month`)
- 6 consultations booked
- 2 credit purchases

That produces 2 customers from 1,000 visitors. At `$3,000` gross profit each, the visitor cohort is worth about `$6,000`, or `$6` per visitor. The tool does not need consumer-scale traffic to be useful if it attracts the right operators.

Channel CAC with $0 paid budget is mostly founder time:

- targeted founder/operator DMs: high effort, maybe `$0` cash CAC, but 2–3 hours per qualified call
- community posts: low cash CAC, lower conversion, useful for learning
- Credex existing network: lowest CAC if Credex can route warm prospects through the audit
- SEO/content: slow, not a week-1 strategy

Profitability threshold: if 300 completed audits produce 6 booked consultations and 2 purchases, the completed-audit-to-consultation rate is 2%, and consultation-to-purchase is 33%. If average gross profit is `$3,000`, Credex can afford meaningful manual follow-up. If consultation-to-purchase falls below 10%, the tool still may be useful, but the high-savings qualification logic needs tightening.

To drive `$1M ARR` in 18 months, ModelMeter would need to become a repeatable acquisition channel, not just a one-off calculator. At `$3,000` yearly gross value per customer, Credex needs roughly 334 converted customers. If 2% of completed audits convert to customers, that implies about 16,700 completed audits over 18 months, or around 930 completed audits/month. That is high but plausible if the tool earns distribution through benchmarks, internal sharing, and Credex's inventory advantage. The big condition: the audit must consistently identify real savings that Credex can actually help capture.
